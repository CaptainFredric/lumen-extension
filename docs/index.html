Workflow

From messy webpage to ready-to-use evidence.

Capture

Remove the page noise before the first pixel is saved.

Sticky layers, floating widgets, lazy-loaded media, and nested scroll surfaces are handled as part of capture, not as cleanup after the fact.

Responsive Snap

Generate the layout set, not just one lucky viewport.

Lumen can produce a presentable desktop, tablet, and mobile bundle from one page so reviews do not depend on whoever happened to be on the right screen size.

Redact

Protect the export before it leaves the browser.

Emails, phone numbers, token-like secrets, and sensitive filled fields can be sanitized directly in the export pipeline so the artifact is safer by default.

Inspect

Extract the page system alongside the bitmap.

The capture is packaged with palette, typography, layout density, hero line, CTA language, and navigation signals so inspiration becomes structured reference.

Archive

Keep a timeline of useful artifacts instead of dead PNGs.

The local-first backend slice already stores capture history. That creates a direct path into sync, scheduled watchlists, and visual diffing.

Mechanics

The details that make the demo honest.

DOM cleanup

Fixed, sticky, and high-z layers are hidden before the stitched pass and restored afterward.

Lazy-load preflight

The page is force-scrolled before capture so deferred media is present when the slices are taken.

Nested scroll support

Lumen detects custom scroll containers and captures against the active scroll root instead of assuming the whole document.

Retina stitcher with tile fallback

Offscreen compositing respects device pixel ratio and falls back to tiled raw exports on very tall pages.

Responsive capture bundle

Desktop, tablet, and mobile viewports can now be generated in one run and archived together.

Local-first backend slice

Demo session state and capture history already exist, which means the road to a real SaaS product is implementation, not invention.

Who pays

Design teams

They need clean references, responsive comparisons, and structured brand cues for mood boards and audits.

QA and developers

They need issue-ready exports, responsive evidence, and safer screenshots when environments include real user data.

Marketers and copywriters

They need swipe files and campaign archives that are easier to search, sort, and present than raw image folders.

Founders and product operators

They care about what changed, what a competitor shipped, and how positioning evolved across real pages.
